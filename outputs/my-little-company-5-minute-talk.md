# My Little Company — five-minute hackathon talk

## Slide 1 — The owner is the operating system (0:00–0:45)

“In a small business, the owner is often the operating system. The pricing rule,
the brand preference, the reason behind a decision—it all lives in one person’s
head or disappears inside old conversations.

That creates a simple but expensive problem: every employee and every AI assistant
has to ask again, and the answer can change depending on who is available.

My Little Company starts with a different promise: explain it once, and your
company remembers.”

**Action:** Advance when you say “remembers.” The scattered fragments on slide 1
become the governed-memory loop on slide 2.

## Slide 2 — Conversation becomes trusted company knowledge (0:45–1:25)

“Chat is only the input. The product is the governed memory loop underneath it.

The owner says something useful during normal work. My Little Company notices what
may be worth keeping, but it does not silently make it company truth. It creates a
clear suggestion with the statement, the reason, the source, and who it affects.

A human approves it. Only then does it enter the Playbook and influence future
work across Marketing, Operations, and employees.

One conversation. One explicit approval. Consistent action everywhere.”

**Action:** Point across the four stages. Pause briefly on the human-approval step.

## Slide 3 — Live challenge (1:25–1:40)

“Let’s teach the company one real rule and see how far it travels.

We’ll start in Marketing, approve the knowledge, turn it into operations, and then
test whether an employee gets the same answer.”

**Action:** Click **Start live demo** or switch to the prepared Chat tab.

## Live demo (1:40–4:35)

### 1. Ask Marketing for help

Enter:

> Tuesdays are quiet. Create a promotion to bring more customers in.

Say:

“This is normal work. The owner is not filling out a knowledge-management form.”

### 2. Teach the company naturally

Enter:

> We never discount more than 15%. We prefer offering a free add-on because we want to maintain a premium image.

Say while the card appears:

“My Little Company heard more than a one-off prompt. It found a durable pricing decision—but it
still is not company truth. The owner can inspect the statement, rationale, roles,
and source, then approve, edit, or ignore it.”

Click **Approve**.

Say:

“Now the company remembers both what was decided and why.”

### 3. Reuse it across the company

Ask Marketing:

> Please revise the Tuesday promotion using our approved company rules.

Point out the complimentary add-on and source chip.

Open **Operations** and click **Generate Tuesday Promotion SOP**.

Say:

“The same approved decision moves from campaign thinking into repeatable front-desk
steps. The SOP is still a suggestion until a human approves it.”

Open **Employee** and ask:

> Can I give a customer 25% off?

Say after the answer appears:

“The employee gets a clear no, the approved 15% limit, the reason, and the source.
The owner does not need to be in the room.”

## Close (4:35–5:00)

“Most AI tools help with one conversation. My Little Company helps the business
become more consistent after that conversation is over.

Chat is the input. Approved company memory is the product.

Explain it once. Your company remembers.”

## Presenter safeguards

- Reset the demo before presenting.
- Keep Chat → Marketing open in a second browser tab.
- Paste prepared prompts instead of typing them live.
- If time is short, skip the revised Marketing response and go directly from approval to Employee.
- If an external service fails, use local deterministic mode and describe it honestly.
